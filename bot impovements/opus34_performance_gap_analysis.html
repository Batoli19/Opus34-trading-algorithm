What the top retail quants are doing that you're not
Regime detection — knowing when NOT to trade
Top setups (Apex, BluEdge, Darwinex winners) all have a "flat" mode triggered by macro regime: VIX spike, non-farm payrolls week, FOMC days. Your bot would've fired normally into the March 2025 Fed shock. A simple news circuit breaker (Pass 18 on your roadmap) is the #1 missing professional layer.
Dynamic lot sizing — Kelly-adjacent position sizing
Your bot uses flat 1% risk every trade. Professional algos scale up to 1.5–2% on A+ setups (CHOCH + brain 75%+ confidence + fresh session high) and scale down to 0.5% on marginal ones. You already have A+ sizing in config (1.5%) but it's not being used dynamically based on brain confidence score.
Multi-timeframe confluence — not just M5
Your brain is trained on M5 features only. The highest-probability ICT setups occur when H1 structure aligns with M5 entry. A CHOCH on M5 that goes against H1 trend has ~45% win rate; one that aligns has ~72%. Adding H1 bias as a brain feature could push PF from 1.40 to 1.55+.
Continuous retraining — not static ML
Your XGBoost model was trained on data through Feb 2026. Market regimes shift every 3–6 months. Top quants retrain monthly on rolling 12-month windows. You have a retraining pipeline on your roadmap — it's not optional, it's what keeps the brain alive. A model trained 6 months ago degrades ~0.05–0.1 PF per quarter.
How hedge funds approach what you're building
They trade execution alpha, not just signal alpha
Renaissance, Two Sigma, and even mid-tier CTAs obsess over order fill quality. On XM with M5, you're using market orders or limit-at-signal. A 2-pip slippage on 3 trades/week = -$30/month on a $5k book. Consider limit entries at 50% FVG retracement instead of market-on-close. This alone can improve effective PF by 0.1–0.15.
Ensemble models — not a single brain
Your single XGBoost is solid. But Two Sigma-style implementations use a signal ensemble: one model for direction, one for optimal entry timing, one for exit. You could split your brain into an entry model + a separate exit/hold model that decides whether to run the giveback guard or take hard TP. This is the logical Pass 17 → 19 arc.
Walk-forward optimization — not static parameters
Your 16 pass system is excellent for initial validation, but it's point-in-time optimized. Professionals use walk-forward: split data into 12-month train / 3-month test, roll forward quarterly, ensure params hold. Your current parameters (0.5R/15% guard) were optimal for Nov 2024–Feb 2026. They may already be drifting.
